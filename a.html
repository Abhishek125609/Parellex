VARANASI


Varanasi, nestled in the northern Indian state of Uttar Pradesh, boasts a rich history dating back to the 11th century B.C. Revered as the spiritual nucleus of India, it magnetizes Hindu pilgrims from far and wide, drawn to the sacred waters of the Ganges River for purifying baths and funeral rituals. Wandering through Varanasi's labyrinthine alleys reveals a tapestry of spirituality, with around 2,000 temples adorning its landscape. Among these architectural marvels stands the eminent Kashi Vishwanath Temple, colloquially known as the "Golden Temple," venerating the Hindu deity Shiva. This sacred edifice exudes a profound aura, attracting devotees seeking blessings and enlightenment.

AYODHYA


Ayodhya was historically known as Saketa. The early Buddhist and Jain canonical texts mention that the religious leaders Gautama Buddha and Mahavira visited and lived in the city. The Jain texts also describe it as the birthplace of five tirthankaras namely, Rishabhanatha, Ajitanatha, Abhinandananatha, Sumatinatha and Anantanatha, and associate it with the legendary Bharata Chakravarti. From the Gupta period onwards, several sources mention Ayodhya and Saketa as the name of the same city.

LEH


Leh, nestled in the Indian Union territory of Ladakh, stands as a vibrant hub in the midst of breathtaking landscapes. Serving as the largest city and one of the joint capitals of Ladakh, Leh is steeped in history and culture. It holds the dual distinction of being the current administrative center of the Leh district and the former capital of the Kingdom of Ladakh.
Perched at an altitude of over 3,500 meters above sea level, Leh boasts a unique blend of Tibetan and Indian influences, evident in its architecture, cuisine, and traditions. The city's strategic location along the ancient Silk Route has facilitated trade and cultural exchange for centuries. With its awe-inspiring monasteries, including the iconic Thiksey and Hemis Monasteries, Leh is a haven for spiritual seekers and adventure enthusiasts alike. The rugged terrain surrounding the city offers ample opportunities for trekking, mountaineering, and exploring pristine natural beauty.
